40 houses on govt land bulldozed in Jewar’s Rabupura, FIRs to follow

Ashni Dhaor / May 12, 2025, 23:47 IST
Share
AA
Text Size
  • Small
  • Medium
  • Large
Follow us
Noida: The district administration has bulldozed 40 houses that came up on a two-kilometre stretch of the irrigation department's drain in Jewar's Rabupura village. Encroachment on gram samaj land in Kulesara was also demolished as a part of the anti-encroachment drive.District magistrate (DM) Manish Verma said FIRs will be registered against encroachers.The action was taken after a report was received from the irrigation department, following which the tehsil team in Jewar identified and marked the illegal structures. The demolition work continued for around 20 days, after which the entire stretch of the drain was cleared.The DM added the clearing of the drain will help prevent waterlogging and improve the local drainage system. The administration will continue monitoring the area to prevent further encroachments. Meanwhile, in a separate action, the Sadar tehsil team removed illegal construction from Gram Samaj land in Kulesara village. The DM said construction activities were being carried out by land mafias on community land, which has now been stopped and demolished. FIRs will be filed against those responsible for the encroachments following an inquiry, and strict action will be taken to prevent the illegal occupation of public land.The administration has also issued warnings to refrain from illegal constructions, adding that violations will invite legal action without exception. Since its establishment in 2017, the anti-land-grabber task force has recovered 168 hectares of encroached government land across Noida, Jewar, and Dadri. Sadar tehsil accounted for the highest portion with 71.79 hectares, followed by Dadri (51.96 hectares) and Jewar (44.35 hectares). Authorities have identified 100 individuals as land mafias and filed 123FIRs against encroachers. Additionally, 68 revenue cases and 15 civil suits have been initiated in courts. Official records show that the dedicated portal has received 3,801 complaints so far, out of which 1,496 were actionable, leading to administrative or legal action. The DM said the portal remains a crucial tool for citizens to report illegal encroachments and help protect public land.
